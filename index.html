Linear Motion Info

Linear motion refers to the movement of an object in a straight line. It is described by the following equations:

  • 1. Distance (s) = Speed (v) × Time (t)

    This equation calculates the distance traveled when speed and time are known.

  • 2. Final Velocity (v) = Initial Velocity (u) + Acceleration (a) × Time (t)

    This equation is used to determine the final velocity of an object based on its initial velocity, acceleration, and time.

  • 3. Distance (s) = Initial Velocity (u) × Time (t) + (1/2) × Acceleration (a) × Time² (t²)

    This equation calculates the total distance traveled when an object is accelerating.

  • 4. Final Velocity² (v²) = Initial Velocity² (u²) + 2 × Acceleration (a) × Distance (s)

    This equation relates the final velocity of an object to its initial velocity, acceleration, and distance traveled.

Key Concepts:

  • Uniform Motion: When an object moves with a constant speed in a straight line.
  • Acceleration: The rate of change of velocity per unit time.
  • Deceleration: Negative acceleration, where an object slows down.

Projectile Motion Info

Projectile motion refers to the motion of an object that is projected into the air and is influenced by gravity. The object follows a curved path known as a trajectory. The motion can be analyzed in two dimensions: horizontal and vertical.

Key Equations:

  • 1. Horizontal Range (R):

    R = (v0² * sin(2θ)) / g

    This equation calculates the horizontal range of the projectile, where v0 is the initial velocity, θ is the launch angle, and g is the acceleration due to gravity.

  • 2. Maximum Height (H):

    H = (v0² * sin²(θ)) / (2g)

    This equation gives the maximum height reached by the projectile.

  • 3. Time of Flight (T):

    T = (2 * v0 * sin(θ)) / g

    This equation calculates the total time the projectile is in the air.

  • 4. Vertical Displacement (y):

    y = v0 * sin(θ) * t - (1/2) * g * t²

    This equation describes the vertical displacement of the projectile at any time t.

Key Concepts:

  • Trajectory: The path followed by a projectile, which is parabolic in shape.
  • Launch Angle: The angle at which the projectile is launched, significantly affecting its range and height.
  • Gravity: The force acting downwards, causing the projectile to follow a curved path.
